Risotto

Description

Risotto is an Italian rice dish cooked with broth until it reaches a creamy consistency. The broth can be derived from meat, fish, or vegetables. Many types of risotto contain butter, onion, white wine, and Parmesan cheese.

Ingredients

  • 400g arbori rice (special risotto rice)
  • 1L vegetable broth
  • 300g boletus of your choice
  • 100g onions
  • 50g butter
  • 100g parmesan
  • 75mL cream
  • 150mL white wine
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper

Steps

  1. Chop the onion in small cubes
  2. Melt the butter in a hot pot with a bit of olive oil
  3. Poach the onion at low heat until translucid
  4. Clean and chop the selected mushroom
  5. Add the boletus to the pot with salt and pepper to liking
  6. Saute for a couple of minutes at medium-high heat until it has reduced to about half its volume
  7. Add the rice and stir vigorously so it doesn't burn
  8. Add the wine and stir until the alcohol has evaporated
  9. While doing so, preheat the broth at a constant temperature without boiling
  10. Turn the heat to medium-low and start by adding the broth in small quantities
  11. Each time the rice looks like it's getting dry, add more broth. Repeat for 15 minutes
  12. By that time, test the cooking of the rice. If it's not fully cooked add more broth and cook until your liking
  13. Add the grated parmesan and the cream. Stir to fully incorporate the ingredients
  14. Let the risotto rest in the pot, covered, so it finishes cooking
  15. When serving, we recommend topping the plate with a few parmesan flakes
